Headings

Research shows that our users are most interested in learning about our statistics and analysis of the data.

Our research has shown that users come to bulletins to complete tasks. Generally, to:

  • learn about new data
  • access new data
  • find out how certain data was collected

    • Bulletin headings separate the different types of content on the page to reflect those user needs, helping users to complete their tasks easily.

    Introducing the sections in a bulletin

    We use the bulletin topic in each section heading to make it clear what each section is about.

    For example, on the "Employment" page of the Labour Market collection, the headings would look like this:

    On this page:

    • Analysis of employment
    • Employment data
    • Glossary
    • Measuring this data
    • Strengths and limitations
    • Find out more
    • You may also be interested in
    • Contact us

    What to write in each section

    Section name Content focuses on
    Analysis of employment
    • Main statistics
    • Commentary about statistics, including essential warnings
    Employment data
    • Summary of data referenced in this bulletin, with links
    • Explains what data is available
    • Explains how detailed the data is
    Glossary
    • Definitions of up to 6 terms used in this bulletin
    Measuring this data
    • Explains where the data comes from
    • Explains how the data is collected
    • Links to methodology articles, user guides, and roadmap
    Strengths and limitations
    • Uncertainty about the data
    • How the data should and shouldn't be used
    • Whether any data could be misused
    • Where multiple sources of data are available, which are better for different purposes
    Find out more
    • Links to more detailed content on this topic, such as technical articles, detailed commentary, or deeper analysis
    You may also be interested in
    • Links to other, related topics on the ONS website
    • Links to related content from elsewhere
    Contact us
    • Name, email address, and phone number for the statistical contact

    Pages in this collection

    Where there’s a user need, we publish a number of bulletins as a “collection”. Users can find their way between these pages using the “Pages in this collection” menu.

    This list of links should:

    • begin with the overview page
    • list the most popular pages first, based on business area understanding to begin with, and using analytics later
    • where it makes more sense to group some pages by topic, rather than popularity, do so (employment should be followed by unemployment, for example)

    For example, all pages in the Labour Market collection would include the following links:

    Pages in this collection:

    • Labour market overview
    • Employment
    • Unemployment
    • Average weekly earnings
    • Public and private sector employment
    • Actual hours worked
    • Workforce jobs
    • Labour disputes
    • Economic inactivity
    • Young people in the labour market
    • Redundancies
    • Vacancies
